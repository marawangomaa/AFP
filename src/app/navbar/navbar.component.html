Introduction

Food is more than flavor; it’s a language of power. Food isn’t just eaten; it’s negotiated, weaponized, and celebrated as cultural currency. In West Africa, dishes like Jollof rice tell stories of togetherness, national pride, friendly rivalry, and global ambition. Gastrodiplomacy is how nations wield cuisine to build influence, from embassy dinners where friendship with the host notion and a positive image are sealed over shared meals to viral Twitter wars over whose Jollof reigns supreme. All those constitute a cultural influence or appeal classified as soft power.
